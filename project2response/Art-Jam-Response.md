I’d like to start with Nou Noune’s project honestly, it might be the besttt one!!!! I’m genuinely impressed by the vision, creativity, and execution. After exploring instagram (linked through a 3D interactive icon button), I learned more about this project. The project guides users through the stories of different protagonists inside a house built in the sims. Viewers can choose and modify a body, creating an experience that both validates and educates trans and non-trans audiences. It’s a brilliant concept.
The experience opens with a small square at the center of the screen a room where you can control the camera using arrow keys shift and ctrl. There are about six interactive elements. Most of them redirect to external pages, but a few are embedded interactions: clicking the first character from the left makes their shirt disappear, the second character cycles through poses every five seconds, and the third sits on a circular black-and-white patterned platform (all the characters are placed on a similar platform). The modeling and overall scene design are incredibly polished and everything looks seamless.
From left to right, the interactive elements are thoughtfully designed. There’s a chart that scales up on hover and links to a testosterone HRT timeline. Next is the Instagram icon leading to the project’s page, where the full creative process is documented. A trans flag on the wall connects to a Quebec trans support page. Then there’s a Google Form inviting trans, non-binary, genderqueer, or Two-Spirit individuals to share their experiences with avatars in gender exploration. The final element which seems to be like a computer(i might be totally wrong) discusses the role of avatars in gaming for trans and gender-diverse youth.
It’s truly inspiring to see peers create work that communicates such a strong message. I’ve been especially interested in how interactive media can be used to spread awareness people have short attention spans, and projects like this are a powerful way to engage them meaningfully.
That said, I do have a couple of critiques. The description mentions that viewers can modify and choose a body, but I was only able to interact with the first character; perhaps this is just an early prototype. I also wish the aspect ratio were larger to make the experience feel more immersive, more like a full simulator. Aside from that, the project is incredibly well executed and really impressive work.

And of course, I had to mention Emile’s bike garage project. It's soooo impressive. I’m really glad he decided to revisit his midterm and expand on it by adding more elements. I had the chance to talk with him about it and see some of the models he created in Blender, along with the animations he implemented. For example, on the home screen, when you hover over “Maintenance,” the toolbar opens up, and when you hover over “Register Part,” one of the shelves on the left slides open. Those details really bring the project to life.

Speaking of revisiting midterms, Kerven and Rayvhaun also built on their earlier project the early windows theme. This time, they included an MP3 player that plays songs alongside album cover I guess, as well as a selfie app, which is a really fun addition. I also had a chance to look through their code and noticed they relied quite heavily on `<div>` elements in their html files; I think they could explore using more classes or attribute based manipulation for cleaner structure. It would also be great if the toolbar in their paint app were interactive. That said, it’s still a very cool and creative project overall.
